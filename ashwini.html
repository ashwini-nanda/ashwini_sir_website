Prof. Ashwini K. Nanda, PhD

Fellow of Computer Society, IEEE (USA) | Fellow of Asia Pacific AI Association

Dr. Ashwini K. Nanda is a globally recognized research scientist and executive specializing in high-performance computer systems, architecture, and artificial intelligence. He currently serves as an Honorary Chair Professor at IIT Guwahati. Furthermore, he is an Adjunct Professor at IIT ISM Dhanbad. He earned his PhD in Computer Science from Texas A&M University, his MTech in Communication Engineering from IIT Madras, and his Bachelor's degree in Electronics Engineering from Sambalpur University. His foundational technologies have powered some of the fastest supercomputers across the USA, Europe, and India.

Academic Leadership & Teaching

  • Served as the Founding Center-Director of the AI and HPC Research Center (AHRC) at IIT Bhubaneswar from 2023 to 2026.
  • Acted as a Visiting Professor at IIT Bhubaneswar between 2023 and 2026.
  • Led the development of 10 applied AI products and prototypes to Technology Readiness Levels 4-7, focusing on agriculture, medicine, and transportation.
  • Taught courses in Computer Architecture, HPC, AI/Machine Learning, and Applied AI.
  • Introduced a new PhD program in Applied AI under the AHRC at IIT Bhubaneswar.
  • Co-supervised PhD students from MIT, USC, UIUC, and the University of Rochester during his time at IBM Research.
  • Co-supervised 5 Post Docs, 4 PhD/MS students, 8 MTech students, and 30 BTech students at IIT Bhubaneswar.

Corporate Leadership & Supercomputing

  • Founder and CEO of HPC Links/HPC Research (USA/India, 2010-Present).
  • Developed Hybrid HPC system software products for leading global clients including Microsoft Research USA, NEC Research USA, Airforce Research Labs USA, Atomic Weapons Establishment UK, and Total Energy France.
  • Co-developed and deployed cybersecurity management software for NPCIL to protect the Reserve Bank of India’s financial data center servers.
  • Deployed cybersecurity solutions for Reliance Industries refineries to prevent cyber-attacks.
  • Served as CEO and Head of Tata Computational Research Laboratories (CRL) in Pune from 2007 to 2010.
  • Built Asia’s fastest supercomputer, ‘Eka’, which was utilized for India’s first lunar mission, Chandrayaan-1.
  • Served as Chief Scientist and Department Head of Hybrid supercomputing systems at IBM TJ Watson Research Center from 1995 to 2008.
  • Pioneered Hybrid CPU/GPU HPC technology at IBM, which catalyzed the modern AI revolution.
  • Led the technology development for ‘Roadrunner’ at Los Alamos National Labs, the world’s first PetaFLOP supercomputer.
  • Invented CPU architecture techniques, including micro-instructions and misprediction recovery cache, as a Senior Research Engineer at Texas Instruments Research from 1993 to 1995.
  • Developed India's first defense parallel computer for the Aakash Missile Program under Hon. Dr. Abdul Kalam at Wipro R&D from 1986 to 1988.
